Frequently Asked Questions

  • Q: How often should I change my car's oil?
    A: It's generally recommended to change your car's oil every 3,000 to 5,000 miles, or as recommended by your vehicle's manufacturer.
  • Q: Why is my check engine light on?
    A: The check engine light can indicate various issues ranging from a loose gas cap to more serious engine problems. It's important to have your vehicle diagnosed by a professional mechanic to determine the exact cause.
  • Q: When should I replace my brake pads?
    A: Brake pads typically need to be replaced every 30,000 to 70,000 miles, depending on driving habits and vehicle usage. However, it's essential to have your brakes inspected regularly to ensure optimal performance and safety.
  • Q: What causes my car to overheat?
    A: Overheating can be caused by a variety of factors, including low coolant levels, a faulty thermostat, a leaking radiator, or a malfunctioning cooling fan. It's crucial to address overheating issues promptly to prevent serious engine damage.
  • Q: How often should I rotate my tires?
    A: Tire rotation is typically recommended every 5,000 to 7,000 miles to ensure even tread wear and extend tire life. Regular tire rotation helps maintain traction, improves fuel efficiency, and enhances overall driving performance.
